## üîÑ Replacing Images

### For Client Projects:

When customizing this website for a client, **replace all stock images** with:
1. Professional photos of their actual restaurant
2. Photos of their real dishes
3. Pictures of their team members
4. Photos of their actual location

### How to Replace:

**Option 1: Local Images** (Recommended)
1. Save client photos in the `images/` folder
2. Name them descriptively (e.g., `tacos-al-pastor.jpg`)
3. Update the HTML to point to local files:
```html
<!-- Change from: -->
<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-xxx" alt="Tacos">

<!-- To: -->
<img src="images/tacos-al-pastor.jpg" alt="Tacos al Pastor">
```

**Option 2: Different Stock Images**
- Visit https://unsplash.com
- Search for specific topics
- Copy the image URL
- Replace in HTML

## üé® Image Optimization Tips

Before using images on a live website:

1. **Resize Images**:
   - Hero images: 1920x1080px
   - Gallery images: 800x600px
   - About section: 600x400px
   - Team photos: 400x400px

2. **Compress Images**:
   - Use https://tinypng.com (free)
   - Or https://squoosh.app (free)
   - Aim for under 200KB per image

3. **Format**:
   - Use JPG for photos
   - Use PNG for graphics with transparency
   - Consider WebP for better compression

4. **File Naming**:
   - Use descriptive names
   - Use hyphens, not spaces
   - Use lowercase
   - Example: `fresh-tacos-closeup.jpg`

## üì∑ Taking Professional Restaurant Photos

### For Best Results:

**Food Photography**:
- Natural lighting is best
- Photograph from 45-degree angle
- Use simple, clean backgrounds
- Show the dish's best features
- Take multiple shots

**Restaurant Interior**:
- Shoot during best lighting
- Show the ambiance
- Keep it clean and organized
- Highlight unique features
- Wide-angle shots work well

**Team Photos**:
- Good lighting
- Consistent background
- Professional attire
- Friendly expressions
- Same camera angle for all

### DIY vs Professional:

**DIY (Budget Option)**:
- Use a good smartphone camera
- Natural lighting
- Clean presentation
- Multiple shots to choose from

**Professional Photographer** (Recommended):
- Higher quality results
- Better composition
- Professional editing
- Consistency across all photos
- Worth the investment
